Travel & Stay

◆

All the information you need to plan your trip and enjoy the weekend with us in Mexico.

Getting There

The nearest airport is Mexico City International Airport (AICM / MEX).

Avoid AIFA — it's farther away and has very limited transport options.

Hacienda San Gabriel de las Palmas is outside Mexico City. You will need to travel from Mexico City to the venue — about 90 minutes south.

We strongly recommend using our weekend shuttles rather than renting a car or taking taxis.

Good to Know
Transport

We will provide shuttles throughout the weekend — we strongly recommend using those.

When moving around Mexico City, use Uber, not taxis from the street.

Safety
  • Use Uber instead of street taxis
  • Do not display phones or valuables openly
  • Avoid taking Ubers alone late at night
  • Use ATMs inside banks or hotels
  • Stick to well-lit areas
  • Share your location when traveling
Food & Water

A small heads up so you can fully enjoy the weekend — "Moctezuma's Revenge" is real, and we'd prefer you not experience it.

  • Avoid drinking tap water
  • Skip ice in drinks
  • Avoid raw fruits and vegetables unless from trusted places
  • Avoid street food (even if tempting)
Currency

Mexico uses the Mexican Peso (MXN). We recommend exchanging some money before or upon arrival.

Have both card and cash available — most venues accept cards, but cash is useful for tips, markets, and smaller spots.

Travel Insurance

Recommended. Covers flights, medical, and luggage — always worth it for international trips.

Connectivity

Install an eSIM before arrival — Airalo works great. Alternatively, buy a SIM card at any OXXO (Mexican convenience stores found everywhere).

Also install WhatsApp — it's the main communication tool in Mexico.
